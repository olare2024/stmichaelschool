Medication Policy

St. Michael School is committed to providing a safe and supportive environment for all students. The administration recognizes that some students may require medication during school hours to manage medical conditions.

General Guidelines

1. Parents or guardians must notify the school in writing about any prescription or over-the-counter medication that needs to be administered during school hours.

2. Medication must be brought to the school office by a parent or guardian. Students are not allowed to carry medication unless specifically authorized in writing by both a parent or guardian and the student's healthcare provider.

3. All medication must be in its original, labeled container, and the label must include the student's name, medication name, dosage, and administration instructions.

Emergency Medication

In the case of emergency medication (e.g., epinephrine auto-injectors, inhalers), a student may be permitted to carry and self-administer the medication if authorized by both a parent or guardian and the student's healthcare provider. The school must be notified in writing of such arrangements.

Communication and Documentation

Regular communication between parents or guardians, healthcare providers, and school staff is essential. The school nurse or designated staff member will keep records of administered medications and notify parents or guardians of any concerns or incidents.

Non-Prescription Medication

Non-prescription medications (e.g., pain relievers, cough medicine) may be administered at the discretion of the school nurse or designated staff, following the guidelines provided by the parent or guardian on the school's Medication Authorization Form.Download PDF

Policy Review and Updates

This Medication Policy is subject to periodic review and updates. Parents or guardians will be notified of any changes to the policy, and updated information will be made available on the school's official website.
